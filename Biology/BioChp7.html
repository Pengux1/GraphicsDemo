Question 1

Briefly describe Pangaea, Laurasia and Gondwana. identify how each relates to the others.

Pangea was a supercontinent, consisting of most of earths landmass. Over a period of about 20 million years, Pangea broke up into two continents, Laurasia and Gondwana. Laurasia contained North America, Asia and Europe, while Godwana contained Antarctica, Australia, New Zealand, India, Africa and South America.

Question 3

Suggest what process might explain how fossils that died on the sea floor could be found high in the Himalayan mountains.

Continental drift, and thus plate techtonics caused the sea floor to be 'pushed' into another landmass, causing the mountain range to form with the fossils inside it.

Question 4

Recount how the level of oxygen in the atmosphere has changed since Earth was formed. How has this change affected the type of organisms - in terms of cell type and cell size - that were able to survive as time progressed?

The level of oxygen in the atmosphere has greatly increased since Earth was formed. This allowed more volitile reactions to occur, which lead to eukaryotic animals with much larger cell size to form, as mitochondria use oxygen to form the energy these larger organisms need.

When an organism dies, it is covered by some sort of sedimentary material, in a short period of time. This stops scavengers from getting to the body, and stops decay long enough for fossilastion to occur (As there is a lack of oxygen for bacteria). Question 4

Palaeontologists have found tracing the evolution of sea jellies ("Jellyfish") to be very challenging. With your knowledge of fossils and the process of fossilisation, suggest why this may be the case.

Jellyfish are mostly water and contain barely and 'hard parts'. Due to this, fossilisation is nearly impossible for Jellyfish bodies, which has led to a lack of fossils, and thus records of past organisms Jellyfish evolved from.

Question 6

What is the difference between gradualism and punctuated equilibrium? Are these patterns of evolution mutually exclusive?

Gradualism states that evolution occurs as a steady, slow divergence of lineages, while Punctuated Equilibrium states that apparent bursts of evolution occur due to a swift change to a new species. The two are not mutually exclusive, and both have been oberseved in the fossil record.

Question 1

List the methods used to determine the age of fossils and give the pros and cons of each.

  • Comparative Dating determines the age of a fossil by observing the composition of nearby rocks. This can be used to estimate the ages of fossils relative to each other, but can be incorrect due to techtonic movement or human activity changing the composition of nearby rock.
  • Radiometric Dating uses the half life of isotopes and concentration of them to determine the age of a fossil. The most common method is Carbon dating, which works by assuming the proportion of carbon-14 in the atmosphere to be constant, so the ratio of carbon 14 to carbon 12 can determine the age of the sample. This however has a greater margin of error the older the object.
  • Electron Spin Resonance measures the properties of electrons in the crystals of minerals, using the magnetism of trapped electrons to determine the amount of radiation the sample has experienced. It can be used to measure ages from a few thousand years to 1 - 2 mya.
  • Luminescence Techniques measure characteristics of minerals within sedimentary rocks. The radiation these minerals can only be released when exposed to heat or light, and thus the light emmited by these minerals is measured when exposed to light. This can date the last exposure of the mineral to heat or light, but cannot provide an stimate for the absolute age of sedimentary rock.

Question 3

List two examples of homologous structures and two examples of analogous structures.

Two homologous structures are lizard scales and the pentadactyl limb in various animals. Two analgous structures are eyes, which many animals have independently developed to perform a similar function, and wings of insects and birds used for flying.

Question 1

Define 'divergent evolution' and give an example of how it has affected the evolution of species.

Divergent Evolution is a pattern of evolution where differences between groups of organisms accumulate to a critical point that leads to speciation, the development of a new species. This is what allows new species to evolve.

Question 2

In a group of species that arose from a common ancestor through divergent evolution, would you expect to see homologous structures or analogous structures?

Homologous structures.

Question 3

Give an example of a situation that leads to adaptive radiation of a species.

A certain resource runs dry, such as a specific plant, which leads to a species developing many different features to survive without this resource.

Question 1

Define 'convergent evolution' and give an example of how it has affected the evolution of species.

Convergent evolution is a pattern that occurs when unrelated organisms evolve similar adaptations in response to their enviroment. This is what causes similar stuctures to evolve despite different ancestors.

Question 2

Would homologous structures be present in organisms that have evolved analgous structures?

Question 3

Name an example of a mass extinction event and how it changed the subsequent profile of organisms on earth.

The Cretaceous mass extinction occured in 65 mya, which caused the demise of the dinosaurs, and the rise of mammels and other organisms which had previously been unable to compete with the dinosaurs..
