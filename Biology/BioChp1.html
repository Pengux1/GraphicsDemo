Question 3

Recount why plasma membranes can be described by a fluid mosaic model.

The Plasma Membrane can be described by a fluid mosaic model due to specialised proteins embedded in the bilayer and the membranes being flexible and able to move.

Question 1

Name four types of membranes proteins.

• Receptor Proteins

• Recognition Proteins

• Transport Proteins

• Adhesion Proteins

Question 2

State the functions of the different types of membrane proteins.

• Receptor Proteins detect molecules by binding them to ligends to incite specific functions.

• Recognition Proteins are markers that identify the cell as belonging to the organism.

• Transport Proteins allow movement of specific substances across a membrane.

• Adhesion Proteins link cells together to maintain tissue function.

Question 1

Outline one similarity and one difference between endocytosis and exocytosis.

Both Endocytosis and Exocytosis use vessicles to transport large molecules through the membrane. However, Endocytosis brings molecules into the cell, and Exocytosis brings them out.

Question 2

Distinguish between endocytosis, phagocytosis and pinocytosis.

Endocytosis brings large molecules into the cell, phagocytosis engulfs solids, and pinocytosis takes in liquid.

Question 1

Explain the role of the nucleus in the secretory pathway.

The nucleus creates mRNA, which goes through the nuclear pores into the cytoplasm.

Question 2

Describe the structure and function of ribosomes.

Ribosomes are small organelles, composed of RNA, and protein. Ribosomes attach to mRNA, and use this as a guide to create polypeptides from amino acids.

Question 3

Describe what the endoplasmic reticulum and Golgi apparatus have in common and describe how they differ functionally.

The Golgi Apparatus and the endoplasmic reticulum are both involved in the secretory pathway, both using vesicles for the transport of substances. However, the GA uses enzymes to 'digest' substances, while the endoplasmic reticulum mainly transports substances.

Question 4

Distinguish between rough ER and smooth ER.

The Rough ER has ribosomes attached to it, while the smooth ER does not.
