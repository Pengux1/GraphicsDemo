A definition of natural selection and all of the steps.

Natural Selection is

An understanding of the processes used to establish genetic relatedness.

An understanding of population genetics including speciation, gene flow, genetic drift and the founder effect.

The ability to critically analyse information from a variety of sources.

Understand the threats to survival associated with a lack of variation.

Understand when and why a lack of variation is not important.

All of the steps involved with speciation.

Explain how you can tell if speciation has occured or not.

Be aware of gene flow and the effect of gene flow on variation, speciation and gene pool stability/change.

Define the term founder effect.

Understand how the founder effect influences variation in a population.

Define the term population bottleneck.

Understand how population bottlenecks influence variation in a population.

Understand how population bottlenecks are associated with genetic drift.

Be able to construct and analyse cladograms (phylogenetic trees).

Be aware of the difference between convergent and divergent evolution and associate homologous and analogous structures with these processes.
