Question 2

List the plant hormones and their major functions.

The plant hormones are:

  • Auxins - Promote shoot growth and inhibit lateral bud growth.
  • Cytokins - Stimulate growth of lateral buds and roots.
  • Gibberelins - Promote vertical growth.
  • Ethylene - Involved in fruit ripening.
  • Abscisic Acid - Promotes dormancy and water retention during drought.

Question 3

Describe the mechanism of phototropism.

In phototropism, plant shoots grow towards the sun or another source of light. Sunlight triggers the breakdown of auxins in cells, so the concentration of auxin along a length of shoot in greater in the side facing away from the sun, causing cell elongation on that side, which then causes the shoot to grow towards the sun.

Question 1

Define what makes a molecule a neurotransmitter and distinguish it from a hormone.

A neruotransmitter is a molecule that is released in response to presynaptic depolarisation, and that its specific receptors are located on the postsynaptic membrane. This distinguishs it from that of a hormone, although some neurotransmitters such as ADH can act as both.

Question 2

Describe the role of the sodium-potassium pump in conveying action potentials along an axon.

The sodium potassium pump polarises the cell by pumping sodium out of the cell, and potassium in, causing the positive charges outside of the cell to become greater than the number inside the cell. This difference allows the cell to be negative compared to its surroundings.

Question 3

Discuss why only a small amount of hormone is required to initate a response in a cell.

The binding of hormones to extracellular receptors stimulates rapid production of a second messenger, which elecits the response. Because many second messengers are made from a hormone binding to a single receptor, small amounts of hormone can iniate a response in a cell.

Question 1

Compare and contrast the disease mechanisms of type 1 and type 2 diabetes.

In type 1 diabetes, the beta cells of the pancreas are destroyed by the immune system and no longer secrete insulin. In type 2 diabetes the insulin receptor in cells no longer responds to the insulin signal, and the patient has become 'insulin resistant'.

Question 2

Explain why the loss of myelin can cause impaired neurotransmission in multiple sclerosis.

The loss of myelin slows down the transmission of nerve impulses and disrupts signals. This can lead to uncoordinated movements, blurred vision and depression.

Question 1

Outline the steps of apoptosis.

In apoptosis, a 'Pro-apoptotic' signal is recieved by the cell, which causes the activation of apoptotic enzymes (Called Caspases) within the cell. Degredation of the neuclus, shrinkage of the cell and blebbing of the cell occur, reducing it into apoptotic bodies, which are then engulfed by phagocytes.

Question 3

Compare the intrinsic and extrinsic pathways of apoptosis and describe the ultimate outcomes of each pathway.

The intrinsic pathway of apoptosis is when apoptosis is defaultly activated when a cell does not receive adequate regulatory signals from its internal enviroments. The cell kills itself because it senses cell stress. The extrinsic pathway of apoptosis occurs from external signals that bind through death receptors. The cell kills itself due to signals from other cells.

Question 2

List three examples of disease that involve apoptosis and describe the nature of the involvement.

Alzheimer's disease involves caspases cleaving the amyloid precursor protein to from a protein which accumulates in plaques to cause neurodegeneration.

Heart Failure involves apoptosis of cardiac muscle cells, which leads to heart failure.

Lupus involves the failure of cells of the immune system that detect a foreign infectious agent to undergo apoptosis unless danger signals are also present. This normally would prevent the proliferation of cells that might attack normal components of the body.

Question 3

Explain how cancer arises with respect to defects in apoptosis.

Cancer arrises when when a cell has acquired mutations in its genome that allow it to evade apoptosis, and continue to survive and divide, typically at an increased rate.
