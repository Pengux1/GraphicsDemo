Question 1

Define 'obligate parasite'.

An obligate parasite is a parasite that is unable to function outside of the host cell, such as a virus.

Question 2

All viruses are pathogens because they are unable to self replicate, and can produce disease.

Question 4

Outline the steps involved for a virus to reproduce.

A virus infects the host cell, injecting its nucleic acid into the cell. Once inside, the viral nucliec acid takes over and directs the host cell to make multiple copies of the viral protein coat and nucleic acid, which are assembled into new viruses. These are then released when the host cell undergoes or lysis, which then spread to other cells, repeating the process.

Question 1

State three ways that a bacterial pathogen can harm its host.

A bacterial pathogen can harm its host by:

  • •Damaging the host tissues directly, due to their rapid division.
  • •Producing toxins that disrupt the functioning of cells nearby or even further away.
  • •Interfering with the hosts immune system, making them susceptible to other pathogens.

Question 2

Describe three effects that follow activation of complement.

•Other complement proteins are activated, which cascades, activating further complement proteins.

•Opsonisation occurs, in which complement proteins bind to the surface of pathogens, acting as a tag to facilitate their dectectionand uptake by phagocytes.

Question 5

Discuss what is involved in the resolution of inflammation and why this is important for limiting tissue damage at sites of infection.

During phagocytosis, macrophages detect and recognise a molecule on the surface of neurophils that have died by apoptosis, stimulating the release of cytokines that promote the resolution of inflammation. The inflammation itself is resolved by multiple factors, including: Stopping leukocytes from going to the site of inflammation, Stimulating the reverse of vasodilation, reducing the permeability of fine blood vessels to the level before inflamation, and stimulating macrophages to safely dispose of material that has accumulated at the site of inflammation. This limits excessive tissue injury and reduces the opportunity for chronic inflammation.
