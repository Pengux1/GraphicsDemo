Question 1

Distinguish between anabolic and catabolic reactions and give an example of each.

An anabolic reaction is one in which atoms and molecules are joined together to make more complex molecules, such as photosynthesis. A catabolic reaction is one that breaks down complex molecules into simpler molecules, such as the breaking down of glucose for cellular energy.

Question 2

Outline two factors that can affect the activity of an enzyme.

Two factors that can affect the activity of an enzyme are:

  • Temperature - Lower temperatures reduce activity and higher temperatures can denature enzymes, making them permanently inactive.
  • pH Levels - Acidic enviroments can cause enzymes to gain hydrogen ions, and alkaline enviroments can cause them to lose hydrogen ions.

Question 2

Distinguish between a non-competitive inhibitor and a competitive inhibitor.

A non-competitive inhibitor is one which does not bind with the enzyme's active site, while a competitive inhibitor binds to the active site, in competition with the substrate for space.

Question 3

Why is arsenic called a 'chronic poison'?

Once arsenic binds to an active site, the normal substrate is permanently excluded. Over time, less and less of the active enzyme remains to catalyse the reactions that produce energy for the cell.

Question 2

Distinguish between the light-dependent and the light-independent stages of photosynthesis in terms of location, requirements and products.

The light independent phase occurs in the stroma of the chloroplast, requires hydrogen ions, CO2 gas, and ATP, and only occurs when light is not present. The light dependent phase occurs in the thylakoid membranes of the choloplasts, requires light, C02, and water, and occurs in the presence of light.
