Summary

The definition of a fuel, including the distinction between fossil fuels and biofuels with reference to origin and renewability (ability of a resource to be replaced by natural processes within a relatively short period of time).

A fuel is a substance with stored energy that can be released relatively easily for use as heat or power.

A fossil fuel is a non-renewable resource that are used faster than they can be replaced. Coal, oil and natural gas fall under this category. Fossil fuels are formed from ancient organisms burried under immense pressure for a large period of time. This process takes millions of years, and thus, fossil fuels cannot be replaed by natural processes in a short period of time.

Biofuels are renewable, derived from plant materals such as grains, sugar can and vegetable waste. As plants are a renewable resource, biofuels are also considered renewable, being able to be replaced in a relatively short period of time(compared to the millions of years that fossil fuel takes).

A Joule is a international standard of measurement for energy.

The chemical energy of a substance is referred to as its energy content. The transformation from one form of energy to another is known as an energy transformation.
