Question 1

Explain the meaning of the term sleep–wake cycle shift.

A sleep-wake cycle shift is the change of timing of the major sleep episode, usually a shift 1 - 2 hours later into the night.

Question 2

Briefly describe the sleep–wake cycle shift that occurs during adolescence.

In adolescences, the release of melatonin is delayed by 1 - 2 hours, thus delaying the onset of sleep by the same time.

Question 3

a. Why is the sleep–wake cycle shift classified as a circadian rhythm phase disorder?

It changes the natural body cycle, pushing it two hours forward.

b. Explain why this type of shift could also be classified as a dyssomnia rather than a parasomnia.

Dyssomina refers to disorders that affecet the quality of sleep, while parasomnia refers to disorders that cause abnormal activity during sleep.

Question 4

Explain why there is a sleep–wake cycle shift during adolescence, ensuring you adopt a biopsychosocial perspective.

From a biological perspective, the onset of puberty causes a delayed release in melatonin, which delays sleepyness. From a social perspective, adolescences usually have commitments during the day, such as school, and thus must fit their social lives into the nighttime, which can cause sleep to be delayed. From a psychological perspective, adolescent brains are undergoing heavy development, and mental disorders that could affect the onset of sleep are much more common in adolescents.

Question 5

a. What is sleep debt?

The sleep that is owed and needs to be made up for becuase daily sleep requirements have not been met.

b. How is it calculated?

The difference between the amount of sleep that is needed to function at an optimal level, and the amount the person actually gets.

c. How can sleep debt adversely impact on the sleep-wake cycle?

An individual will sleep longer to make up for this debt, which can cause the onset of tiredness to be delayed due to the later waking time.

Question 6

What are two potential consequences of the adolescent sleep–wake shift on sleep patterns or activity, other than sleep debt?

An adolesent will stay up later due to the shift, which will cause them to get less sleep than they would otherwise need. This lack of sleep could cause a lack of attention, lethargy or other symptoms.

Question 7

To what extent may it be possible to readjust or compensate for the adolescent sleep–wake shift? Explain your answer.

Daily activities could be pushed later into the day to account for the later sleep times adolescents have.
