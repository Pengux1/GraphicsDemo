Term Definition Example
Addition A sound is added where it previously didn't exist. 'Balloo - un' for balloon.
Adjacency Pair Expected responses to a particular phrase. R: "How are you?"
G: "Good, you?"
Assimilation The process by which sounds become more like each other. "Surprise" to "Suprise"
Fun fact, I used to spell it like "suprise".
Backchanneling Listening noises or paralinguistic gestures that show interest from the listener. "Ooooh, mmmm."
Cede the floor Ending your turn to either hand it to someone or to finish your thoughts. "I hand this conversation to you good chum."
Closing conversation Ending a discourse. "Alright, get out of my sight."
Colloquialism Slang understood by the majority of native speakers of a language. "Cromulent"
Co-operative Signals I'm scared, i'm moving on(Don't know).
Deference Language that shows respect to or acknowledges a high rank or superiority. "Jokes Miss."
Deictic / Deixis Inferred meaning of time, place or person. "Who is he?"
Diminutive Shortening a word and adding a suffix onto the end. "Tradies"
Discourse Particles Short utterances that only occur in conversations. "like, yeah."
Disfluency A mistake made by a speaker that leads to overlap, or breaks the rules of turn taking.
Self-Initiated Self-Repair When a speaker recognises their mistake and repairs it themselves. D: "Yeah Global Politics is a legitimate subject"
D: "Wait, no it isn't."
Shortening Removing complexities from a word to form a new word. "Wimp" from Whimper.
Slang Informal lexical items that are usually associated with a particular group. "Fam"
Tag questions Requests the speaker to express agreement or disagreement from the listener. "Are you even listening?"
Take the floor Getting your turn in a conversation either by following order or by forcefully taking control of it. "Alright, enough talk from you, I'm taking the floor."
Terms of Address A word or phrase used to refer to someone or something without using his, her, or its name. "Your Highness"
Topic Avoidance When someone avoids the topic being asked, usually they do this by changing the topic or going back to something else that they want to talk about "Do you know what is murder? THAT FOOTBALL GAME LAST NIGHT DID YOU SEE IT?"
Topic Change A complete change in topic. S: "Nice weather isn't it? D: "I like tacos."
Topic Development When someone explores a topic further, by asking more in depth questions to get deeper answers or someone else contributes to the conversation to develop the topic further. "Do you like jazz?"
Topic Initialisation Starting a new topic. "How about those animes my fellow memesters?"
Topic Shift Slightly changing the topic to a new topic that is related to the the old one. "Speaking of terrible things, how about that VCE chums?"
Turn Taking / Turn Taking Rules Following context-dependant rules about how the discourse will go. Usually made up of taking the floor, holding the floor and passing the floor, in that order.
Pre-closings A sentence or phrase that shows the end of a discourse is near. "Good heavens will you look at the time!"
Pre-question A sentence or phrase that indicates a question is coming, but is usually a question itself. "Can I hear your opinions on something?"
Prosodic Features Parts of speech which deal with the way in which words, phrases and sounds are articulated, rather than what they mean. Consists of Pitch, Intonation, Stress, Volume, Tempo. "I ^hate physics!"
Pun Play on words or sounds which contains more than one meaning, typically for comedy. http://onelinefun.com/puns/

Go on, treat yourself.
Rapport Talk Social conversation which builds relationships between speakers. R: "How 'bout that weather?", D: "Man it's pretty dank."
Reduction Dropping the endings (and sometimes beginnings) of a word to create a shorter form. "Pants" short for Pantaloons.
Reduplication Following a word with a rhyming nonsense word, or the same word. "That's completely cray-cray bae-bae."
Reflexive Pronouns Pronouns that refer back to the subject of the sentence. "I hate myself."
Relevance Relevance of discussion to the topic at hand. Talking about bears - "I sure do love marsupials."
Repair When the speaker corrects themselves "It is, completely ... partially correct."
Repetition Repeated use of words or phrases in a text. 5 DOLLARS, THAT'S RIGHT THIS OFFER IS FOR A LIMITED TIME ONLY, 5 DOLLARS! YOU BETTER GET IN QUICK BECAUSE THEY'RE WALKING OUT THE DOOR.
Rising Intonation / HRT A rising intonation at the end of statement, so the statement almost sounds like a question. "You know how it is /"
Self-Initiated Other-Repair When a speaker indicates an issue and the listener repairs it. D: "Yeah Global Politics is a legitimate subject"
D: "Wait, no it't not"
S: "Yeah it isn't."
Dropping Intonation / Falling Intonation How the voice falls on the final syllable of a word or phrase. "And that's the way the news goes \"
Echoing When the listener repeats a word or phrase to the speaker to indicate confusion. E: "I like jazz" R: "Jazz?"
Ellison Slurring or omission of certain vowels and consonants in connected speech. "Straya" from Australia.
Ellipsis Removal of words, phrases or clauses that can be inferred from the context or are previously stated. "They have been dancing and h̶a̶v̶e̶ ̶b̶e̶e̶n̶ singing all week."
False Start When a speaker starts to say something, then initiates a repair due to saying something wrong. "I am (.), ah, I was the best driver in this whole damn circuit."
Filler Meaningless words, or sounds that make a pause or hesitation in a conversation. "But-um"
Fixed Phrase A set group of words often used in a particular situation.
Hedging Avoiding a direct response to soften any possible responses. "I think he's alive.
Idiom Expression, word or phrase with a meaning that cannot be inferred from the individual meanings of the words which make it. "He got the rough end of the pineapple."
Interrogative Tags A question at the end of a sentence that seeks confirmation. "The weather's nice, isn't it?
Maintain the floor Attempting to maintain dominance over a conversation. "Yeah, sure, um, uh, that tends to be the case."
Metaphor A non-literal description of one thing as another. "She is fishing in troubled waters."
Minimal Response The listener of a conversation making small noises or worded responses whilst another is talking. "Mmmm, uh-huh"
Negative Face The desire to be recognised as an individual; to be accepted.
Negative Politeness Regard of the right of the addressee to be an individual. "Sorry to bother you sir..."
Non-Fluency Features Various features of spoken text that either indicate a lack of fluency in spoken English, or to create dramatic effect or highlight a specific word or phrase. "I am agree with you on this."
Non-Standard Grammar Grammatical features that are not used the way in which they 'should' be used. "I aint never seen nothing like this chum."
Non-Verbal Feedback Feedback from a listener that doesn’t make use of speech in a spoken conversation. "Hahahahahaha"
Other-Initiated Other-Repair When a listener indicates an issue and repairs it. D: "Yeah Global Politics is a legitimate subject"
S: "Wait, no it't not"
D: "I've been had again, drats."
Other-Initiated Self-Repair When a listener indicates an issue and the speaker repairs it. D: "Yeah Global Politics is a legitimate subject"
S: "Are you sure?"
D: "You're right, it isn't."
Overlap Attempting to take the floor or help the speaker who has the floor. "I think its really (.) [difficult]"
Paralinguistic Features Features of spoken communication that do not involve words. Body movements.
Pause Stopping in the middle of speech. "Man, I do love (..) homework"
Positive Face The need to be accepted or liked by others or to be treated as a member of a group.
Positive Politeness Use of inclusive language that reinforces membership and sense of belonging, increasing positive face. "We should tackle this as a team chum!"
Term Definition Example
Alliteration The repetition of an initial consonant sound, in either consecutive words or words in close proximity. "An Education in Alliteration."
Assonance Repetition of identical vowel sounds in words. "Oh no"
Consonance The reperition of consonant sound in words. "Litter latter."
Onomatopoeia A word formed by the imitation of sound. "Bang."
Rhythm The pattern of stressed and unstressed syllables in speech. "A sailor went to sea, sea, sea."
Rhyme The repetition of word endings that have the same or similar vowel and consonant sounds. "Tight" and "Light"
Anthesis Technique of placing two words or ideas in opposition." "It was the best of times, it was the BLURST of times."
Collocation Some words that are more likely to occur near each other. "Terms and Conditions."
Listing The use of a sequence or list of words of the same type and context. "I like apples, pears and bananas."
Parallelism The repetition of the same syntactic construction. "Easy come, easy go."
Acronym Wprds that are formed by using the first letter of a series of words, prouncing them as a whole word. "ROFL."
Blend New words produced by using parts of two words to create a new one. "Emotion + Icon = Emoticon."
Compounding Creating new words by putting two free morphemes together. "Football."
Contraction Abbreviation of two existing words that can be pronounced. "Can't."
Diminutives Shortenings where a suffix is added to the end of a shortened word. "Tradies."
Initialism The beginning letters in a sequence of words, but continue to be said as a series of letters. "RSPCA."
Neologism A new word, expression or usage of a word. "Gladwrap."
Shortening Dropping the endings or beginnings from a word to create a shorter form. "Emoji" from "Emoticon"
Cliché A phrase or opinion that is overused and betrays a lack of original thought. "A woman's place is in the home."
Idiom A fixed expression that cannot be understood on the basis of the words it contains. "Pot calling the kettle black."
Irony Use of words to xperss something different and often opposite to their literal meaning. "Delicious meal" to a plate of garbage.
Lexical Ambiguity A sentence that can be interpreted in more than one way as a result of lexical choices. "He wanted a treat."
Metaphor Use of language in which one thing is described in terms of another." "As strong as an army."
Oxymoron A phrase that contains two contradictory words placed together." "A fast turtle."
Personification / Animation A term that prescribes human qualities to something non-human. "The door groaned."
Puns A play on words, usually intended to be humorous. "Orange you glad I didn't say orange!"
Simile Use of language that creates a comparison between two things. "As fast as a tiger."
End-Focus When the most important part of a sentence is placed at the end. The house will have to be vacated, by Tuesday."
Front-Focus When the most important part of a sentence is placed at the begining. "By Tuesday, the house will have to be vacated."
Given-New A known appears in the begining, then is followed by new information. "The sky is blue, because of Reghliegh scattering."
It-Cleft A construction in which a single clause has been split into two sections, each with its own verb. "Her parents never visited her in Montreal. It was her mother that always came up with excuses."
Passive Denoting a voice of verbs in which the subject undergoes the action of the verb. "They were killed." as opposed to the active form "He killed them."
Wh-Cleft A subordinate clause that's introduced by one of the wh-wrds (What, who, which, when, where, why, how). "I knew that Jorge was happy, and I thought I knew what was on his mind."
Anaphora A word that refers to something earlier in the text. "Harry is a tradie, He likes cake."
Cataphora A word that refers to something later in the text. "That's a bloody outrage, the fire is."
Coherence The text being logical and consistent.
Cohesion The linguistic links in the text that binds parts of the text together.
Connotation The figerative meaning of a word; the idea behind it. "White flag", A symbol of surrender.
Denotation The literal meaning of a word. "Cat - A feline animal with four legs and a tail."
Hypnomy Subcategories of a higher category. "Fork and spoon are a subsection of cutlery."
Inference Use of prior knowledge to make sense of a text that doesn't explicitly state the meaning. "I can't cross the road without a green light."
Linking Adverbs and Conjunctions They connect two independent clauses or sentences, providing transition between ideas. Furthermore.
Repeition Repeating something that has already been said or written. "A sailor went to sea, sea, sea."
Substitution Use of a different lexical item or expression in place of another one. "Dane is a nerd, he is lame xd."
